You're late, but they've already started clapping, so it doesn't matter.

The fiddler is halfway through a tune when you step into the yard. He sees you, shifts just enough. You come in on his second line. No need to announce yourself.

The reed is dry. First note cracks a little. Good. That gets their heads up.

You move as you play. No point standing still. Close to the bride first — always. Let the sound sit near her face, then carry it out into the people. The ground is uneven. Someone bumps you. You lean and keep the phrase.

It's a tune you know, but not like this. Never like this. The fiddler holds it straight. You bend it. Stretch a note past where it should go. Drop one out and let them feel it missing.

They're already forming the circle. Feet low, not lifting. You push the tempo a touch. Not too much. They follow if you don't force it.

Last week in town, there was a radio in the back of a shop. You stood outside with two others, pretending not to listen. Something came through — American. Clarinet, but not like yours. Faster. Jumping the beat, then landing ahead of it. You didn't understand it, not really. But it stayed.

You try a turn like that now. Just once. Let the phrase slip forward, then catch it.

A few of them look up. Not confused. Just… noticing.

Good.

The bride's father is watching. You shift the tune — ease it into something he knows better. He nods once. That's enough.

You don't think about where the music comes from. Not while you're playing. It's here now. In your hands, in your breath, in the way they move when you lean into a note and don't let it settle.

The circle tightens. Someone nearly falls. Laughter breaks through and you ride it, push higher, let the sound strain a little before it opens.

For a moment, it holds — all of them, the yard, the night, the tune. Then it breaks, the way things that hold completely always break.

You drop out, wipe the mouthpiece, step back in.

It's not yours. You know that.

But tonight it moved through you, and you bent it, and it was not quite the same tune it was before you touched it.
